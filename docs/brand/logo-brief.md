# Hope of Glory Ministry — Logo Brief

> Brand essence: Reverent, luminous, scriptural, royal. Glory, light, the earth, the nations, the cross. Christ-centered. NOT cheesy, NOT gimmicky, NOT lo-fi, NOT clipart.

## Locked palette reference

| Token | Hex | Role in logo |
|---|---|---|
| Glory Gold | `#D4AF37` | Primary mark, cross, sunburst, type accent |
| Deep Heaven Blue | `#0B1F3A` | Primary background, deep-context lockup |
| Midnight Navy | `#050B18` | Reverse-context / favicon background |
| Warm Light | `#FFF8E7` | Inverted wordmark on dark, halo glow |
| Blood Crimson | `#8A1C1C` | Reserved — not used in primary logo |
| Living Olive | `#556B2F` | Reserved — not used in primary logo |

## Type lockup (all three concepts)

- Wordmark: **HOPE OF GLORY** — Cormorant Garamond SemiBold, all caps
- Optional descender line: **MINISTRY** — Inter Medium, all caps, +200 tracking, ~22% of wordmark cap height
- Optical kerning, generous letterspacing on the wordmark (+80 to +120 tracking)

## Concept A — "Radiant Cross Over Horizon" (PRIMARY RECOMMENDATION)

### Visual description

A single, slender Latin cross rendered in Glory Gold floats above a thin horizon line. From the intersection of the cross arms, a soft radial glow emanates — eight to twelve hair-thin rays, longest along the horizontal axis, fading into Deep Heaven Blue. Beneath the cross sits a delicate horizon line (a single 1px–2px gold stroke, broken at its center by the foot of the cross). Below the horizon, the wordmark **HOPE OF GLORY** in Cormorant Garamond, gold on deep blue (or deep blue on warm light for light contexts). A small **MINISTRY** sublabel sits centered beneath.

Proportions: cross height = wordmark cap height × 2.0. Horizon line spans wordmark width × 0.9. Total mark height = ~3.2× cap height.

### Symbolism

- **Cross** — the gospel, the finished work of Christ, the only ground of glory (Galatians 6:14)
- **Horizon** — "the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the Lord, as the waters cover the sea" (Habakkuk 2:14)
- **Light rays** — "the glory of the Lord is risen upon thee" (Isaiah 60:1)
- **Vertical axis** — heaven descending to earth; "thy kingdom come"

### Where it works

| Context | Notes |
|---|---|
| Favicon (16, 32, 48, 180, 512) | Use cross + rays only, drop wordmark. Glory Gold on Midnight Navy. |
| Social avatar (square) | Cross + rays in circular Deep Heaven Blue field with subtle gold edge ring. |
| OBS lower-third | Horizontal lockup, wordmark only with tiny cross glyph as separator. |
| Sermon card watermark | Bottom-right corner at 18% opacity, gold on dark. |
| Email header | Full lockup centered, 320px wide, on Warm Light or Deep Heaven Blue. |
| Print / large-format | Scales to billboard without artifact — vector only, no raster glow. |

### Pros / Cons

**Pros:** Immediately Christian without being aggressive. The cross + horizon + light triad reads as "glory of the Lord covering the earth" — directly scriptural. Works at every size because the cross is the dominant identifier. Distinct from megachurch logo conventions (no swooshes, no gradients, no globes).

**Cons:** Cross may be a barrier in some unreached-people-group contexts (acceptable trade — this is a Christ-centered ministry). The horizon line is fragile at sub-32px sizes and should be dropped from the favicon variant.

## Concept B — "Sunburst Medallion" (SECONDARY OPTION)

### Visual description

A circular gold medallion rendered as a stylized dawn: a half-disc sun rising from a flat horizon, radiating twelve evenly-spaced rays (some long, some short, alternating — Aztec calendar restraint, not flame burst). Contained within a thin gold ring. Wordmark **HOPE OF GLORY** sits centered below the medallion in Cormorant Garamond. The mark contains **no cross** — it is the dawn-over-the-earth motif standalone.

Proportions: medallion diameter = wordmark width × 0.45. Ring stroke = medallion diameter / 80.

### Symbolism

- **Rising sun** — "the dayspring from on high hath visited us" (Luke 1:78); "the sun of righteousness arise with healing in his wings" (Malachi 4:2)
- **Twelve rays** — twelve tribes, twelve apostles, the New Jerusalem
- **Containing ring** — covenant, eternity, the unbroken faithfulness of God
- **Horizon inside the medallion** — Christ as the dawn over the nations

### Where it works

| Context | Notes |
|---|---|
| Favicon | Medallion only, no wordmark. Strong at all sizes including 16×16. |
| Social avatar | Medallion centered on Deep Heaven Blue. Excellent recognition at thumbnail. |
| OBS lower-third | Medallion + horizontal wordmark, medallion at left as anchor. |
| Sermon card watermark | Medallion only, gold on dark, 24% opacity. |
| Email header | Full lockup, medallion + wordmark stacked or horizontal. |
| Merch / embroidery | The medallion embroiders beautifully — no thin lines to break down. |

### Pros / Cons

**Pros:** Universally legible regardless of viewer's faith context — works in apologetics-to-skeptic contexts where a cross might raise defenses prematurely. Strongest favicon and merch performance of the three concepts. Embroiders, etches, and foil-stamps cleanly.

**Cons:** Without the cross, the mark is not visibly Christian on first glance — could be mistaken for a generic "hope" or even solar/new-age brand without the wordmark context. Less scripturally explicit than Concept A.

## Concept C — "Pure Wordmark" (UTILITY / EVERGREEN)

### Visual description

Typography only. **HOPE OF GLORY** set in Cormorant Garamond SemiBold, all caps, generously letterspaced (+120 tracking), rendered in Glory Gold on Deep Heaven Blue. Beneath the wordmark, separated by a single hairline gold rule, sits **MINISTRY** in Inter Medium all caps, +300 tracking, at ~22% the cap height of the wordmark. No glyph. No ornament. Just the words.

Optional refinement: the "O" letters can be subtly weighted as perfect circles to give the mark internal rhythm. The negative space between letters does the work — the wordmark itself becomes the light.

### Symbolism

- **Word-only** — "In the beginning was the Word" (John 1:1). The ministry is a media ministry; the Word is the brand.
- **Letterspacing** — air, breath, room for the Spirit; the letters do not crowd one another
- **Gold on deep blue** — heaven and crown, glory and throne

### Where it works

| Context | Notes |
|---|---|
| Favicon | Use the letters "HG" stacked or a single ornamental "H" — needs a dedicated favicon glyph derived from this lockup. |
| Social avatar | Stacked "HOPE / OF / GLORY" on Deep Heaven Blue, three lines. |
| OBS lower-third | Horizontal wordmark only — fits any aspect ratio cleanly. |
| Sermon card watermark | Tiny wordmark, gold at 22% opacity, bottom-center. |
| Email header | Wordmark centered on Warm Light. The cleanest, most editorial option. |
| Editorial / longform | Pairs beautifully with longform written content — Substack, devotional emails, theology essays. |

### Pros / Cons

**Pros:** Timeless. Never dates. Strongest editorial feel of the three. Reproduces perfectly at any size and in any medium (print, embroidery, foil, screen, embossing). No symbol to argue about theologically.

**Cons:** No favicon-native glyph — requires a derived "HG" monogram for small-square contexts. Recognition relies entirely on the words, which is slower than a symbol at thumbnail scale. Less immediate "Christian" signal than A.

## Recommendation

**Primary lockup:** Concept A (Radiant Cross Over Horizon) — fullest scriptural symbolism, distinctly Christian, distinctly *Hope of Glory*.

**Companion mark:** Concept B (Sunburst Medallion) — used as the favicon glyph, social avatar, and merch mark where the wordmark cannot live.

**Editorial / longform contexts:** Concept C (Pure Wordmark) — devotional emails, Substack, theology essays, footer signatures.

All three concepts share the same wordmark typography (Cormorant Garamond SemiBold), the same palette, and the same proportional system, so they function as one family.

---

## Construction grid (applies to all three)

- Cap height of wordmark = `1x`
- Glyph height (cross or medallion) = `2x`
- Clearspace around full lockup = minimum `1x` on all sides
- Minimum lockup width (full): 160px
- Minimum lockup width (glyph only): 24px (Concept A favicon variant), 16px (Concept B favicon)
- Stroke weight for cross / medallion ring = glyph height / 60
