# Correction Policy

Hope of Glory will make mistakes. Some will be small (a misquoted reference, a date, a name). Some will be larger (a teaching that, on review, misrepresented Scripture or a Christian tradition; an AI response that violated one of this ministry's published policies). This document defines how those errors are acknowledged and corrected.

## Governing Principles

1. **Truth is more important than reputation.** If the ministry has erred, the ministry says so. We do not edit the record silently and pretend we were always right.
2. **Corrections are public, not buried.** A corrected error is logged on the public corrections page so the same record that carried the error carries the correction.
3. **Speed matters, but accuracy matters more.** A correction is published promptly, but only after the ministry is reasonably confident of what the correct statement is.
4. **The correction speaks for itself.** It does not blame the audience, the platform, the AI, or any third party. The ministry takes responsibility for what it publishes.

## The Corrections Page

The ministry maintains a public `/corrections` page. Every published correction lives there. Entries on that page include:

- The date the original statement was published.
- A clear description of what was said that was incorrect, or a quotation of it.
- A clear description of what is correct.
- The date of the correction.
- A short note on how the error happened, when this is known and useful.
- Where appropriate, links to the original piece (with an in-place note indicating it has been corrected) and to relevant sources.

Older entries are not removed. The corrections trail is cumulative. A reader can scroll back and see every acknowledged error this ministry has made.

## What Counts as an Error That Must Be Corrected

The following always warrant a correction:

- A misquotation or misattribution of Scripture.
- A factual error about church history, a historical figure, a doctrinal tradition, or an event.
- A misrepresentation of another Christian tradition or non-Christian view (a steelman failure).
- An AI response that violated a published policy in `doctrine/` (e.g., breached the crisis policy, violated the apologetics policy, claimed authority forbidden by `ai-boundaries.md`).
- A statement that turned out to be misleading even if narrowly defensible.

Minor typographical errors are fixed in place and do not require a corrections-page entry, unless the typo changed the meaning.

## What Does Not Count As an "Error" Requiring Correction

- A teaching that someone disagrees with on a disputed secondary matter the ministry intentionally did not adjudicate (see `disputed-doctrines.md`).
- A teaching that offends a reader without being wrong.
- A user-reported "error" that, on examination, is not actually an error. In these cases, the ministry may publish a clarification rather than a correction.

The distinction matters: a correction concedes error. A clarification simply explains. Both are appropriate in their place; they are not the same.

## AI-Specific Corrections

When the AI gives a wrong answer:

1. If the AI realizes it within the same conversation, it corrects itself plainly: "I was wrong about that. The correct statement is..."
2. If a user reports an error after the fact, the ministry reviews the transcript (with appropriate privacy handling) and, where the error is confirmed, publishes a correction on the corrections page.
3. Recurrent errors of the same kind trigger a review of the underlying policy or prompt. The fix is documented.

The AI does not pretend to have been right when it was wrong. It does not blame the user, "context," or anything else for its mistake.

## Transparency About Process

The ministry discloses, in plain language, how corrections decisions are made and who makes them. Where possible, corrections specify whether the error originated in published written material, in an AI response, or in a presentation, and who reviewed the correction.

The ministry does not promise that every challenge will result in a correction. It promises that every challenge will be considered honestly and that, when the ministry is wrong, it will say so on the record.

## Posture

A ministry that cannot say "we were wrong" cannot be trusted to say anything else. Corrections are not a weakness in the ministry's witness; they are part of it. The Word of God is unchanging. We are not.
