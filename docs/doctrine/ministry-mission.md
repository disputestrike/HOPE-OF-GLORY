# Ministry Mission

## Mission Statement

Hope of Glory Ministry exists to fill the earth with the knowledge of the glory of the Lord by teaching the Scriptures faithfully, lifting up Jesus Christ as the hope of glory in every person who receives Him, and serving the global church in its work of making disciples until the whole earth is filled with His glory.

## The Three Anchors

Three passages of Scripture hold this ministry together. They are not slogans. They define what we exist to do and what success looks like.

### Habakkuk 2:14 — The Earth Will Be Filled With the Knowledge

"For the earth will be filled with the knowledge of the glory of Yahweh, as the waters cover the sea" (WEB).

This is the promise. It is not aspiration; it is prophecy. The glory of God will fill the earth — not as a vague atmosphere, but as knowledge: definite, teachable, capable of being received and passed on. Hope of Glory exists to participate in that filling by making the knowledge of His glory accessible, clear, and grounded in Scripture, to as many people, in as many places, in as many languages, as God enables.

### Psalm 72:19 — Let the Whole Earth Be Filled

"Blessed be his glorious name forever! Let the whole earth be filled with his glory! Amen and amen" (WEB).

What Habakkuk declares as future, Psalm 72 cries as prayer. We do not work toward Habakkuk 2:14 with cold confidence; we pray toward it. Every act of teaching, every line of content, every prayer offered through this ministry is offered with the cry of Psalm 72:19 underneath it. We are not the cause of the earth being filled. We are servants asking, and laboring, that it would be.

### Colossians 1:27 — Christ In You, The Hope of Glory

"To whom God was pleased to make known what are the riches of the glory of this mystery among the Gentiles, which is Christ in you, the hope of glory" (WEB).

The glory that will fill the earth is not abstract. It is the glory of a Person, and that Person comes to dwell in those who trust Him. Hope of Glory takes its name from this verse. Every teaching points back to Christ. Every interaction is held up against the question: Does this exalt Christ, or does it exalt us? If a thing exalts the ministry over the Master, the thing is wrong.

## Theological Vision

These three anchors form a single arc. Habakkuk declares the destination. The Psalm prays toward it. Colossians names the means: a Person, dwelling in His people. The whole earth will be filled with the glory of God because Christ is being formed in every nation that receives Him.

The book of Revelation is a major teaching pillar within this vision, because Revelation shows the consummation of what Habakkuk and the Psalm anticipate: the Lamb who was slain, reigning, and a renewed creation in which the glory of God is its light. Revelation is taught here as a Christ-centered book of hope and worship, not as a calendar of fear.

## Scope

Hope of Glory is a media and teaching ministry. We publish, teach, answer questions, and pray. We do not replace the local church, administer sacraments, ordain ministers, or claim authority over any believer's conscience.

Specifically, we aim to:

1. Teach the Scriptures clearly, in the voice of the historic Christian faith.
2. Make biblical theology accessible to people who lack access to sound teaching.
3. Treat questions seriously, including hard questions and objections.
4. Honor the local church as the primary place where disciples are formed.
5. Refuse manipulation, sensationalism, and any tactic that trades the dignity of the hearer for the growth of the ministry.

## What We Are Not

We are not a prophetic office. We do not predict dates, name modern figures as biblical end-time characters, or claim fresh revelation. We are not a substitute for pastoral care, professional counseling, or emergency services. We are not an authority over any person's salvation, calling, or relationship with God.

## The Measure of Success

The measure of this ministry is not reach, revenue, or recognition. The measure is faithfulness to the Word of God and fruitfulness in the formation of disciples who walk with Jesus Christ. If the earth is filled, even by a hand's breadth, with the knowledge of His glory because this ministry was faithful, that is enough.
