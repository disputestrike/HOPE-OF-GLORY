# Nonprofit formation (D.C.)

Hope of Glory Ministry is incorporating as a religious nonprofit corporation in the District of
Columbia, then applying for federal 501(c)(3) status. This document is a checklist — not legal
advice. Engage a nonprofit attorney before filing.

## Step 1 — Form the entity (D.C.)

- File **Articles of Incorporation for a Nonprofit Corporation** with the D.C. Department of
  Licensing and Consumer Protection (DLCP) Corporations Division.
- Required:
  - Name: Hope of Glory Ministry (run the name-availability search first)
  - Statement of purpose: religious — to fill the earth with the knowledge of the glory of the
    Lord through AI-powered Christian teaching, prayer, apologetics, and digital ministry
  - Registered agent: D.C. resident with physical address, OR a registered agent service
  - At least one incorporator (D.C. allows one)
  - Filing fee
- Submit online via CorpOnline.

## Step 2 — Get an EIN (free, instant)

- Apply at https://irs.gov/ein
- Choose "View Additional Types, Including Tax-Exempt and Government Entities" → "Church or Church-Controlled Organization" if applicable, else "Religious Organization"
- Save the EIN confirmation letter (CP 575). You'll need it for everything else.

## Step 3 — Charitable solicitation registration (D.C.)

- D.C. requires nonprofits to register before soliciting donations from D.C. residents.
- Register with the D.C. Office of the Attorney General, Charitable Trust Section.
- This is separate from incorporation and from federal exemption.

## Step 4 — Federal 501(c)(3) (IRS Form 1023 or 1023-EZ)

- **Form 1023-EZ** if expected gross receipts under $50,000 for next 3 years AND assets under $250,000.
  Streamlined, online, ~3-month turnaround. $275 fee.
- **Form 1023 (long form)** otherwise. ~6-12 month turnaround. $600 fee.
- Draft narrative is at `docs/legal/irs-1023-narrative-draft.md`.
- Required attachments:
  - Articles of Incorporation
  - Bylaws
  - Conflict of Interest Policy (IRS provides a sample)
  - Statement of Faith (use `docs/doctrine/statement-of-faith.md`)
  - Detailed activities description
  - Projected revenue and expenses (3 years)

## Step 5 — Bylaws and Conflict of Interest Policy

- Adopt bylaws at the first board meeting.
- Adopt a conflict-of-interest policy (IRS provides a model).
- File minutes of the first board meeting.

## Step 6 — D.C. tax exemption (after federal approval)

- Apply for D.C. franchise tax exemption with the D.C. Office of Tax and Revenue after
  the IRS 501(c)(3) determination letter arrives.

## Step 7 — Annual filings

- **IRS Form 990-N** (e-Postcard) if gross receipts under $50,000
- **IRS Form 990-EZ** if between $50,000 and $200,000
- **IRS Form 990** if over $200,000
- D.C. biennial report to DLCP
- Annual charitable solicitation renewal

## Important reminders

- 501(c)(3) status is **retroactive to incorporation** if Form 1023 is filed within 27 months
  of formation. File early to preserve retroactive coverage for early donations.
- Until 501(c)(3) is approved, donations are NOT tax-deductible. The /give page reflects this.
- No political campaigning. No substantial lobbying. Read the IRS guidance carefully.
