# The Revelation Desk

The book of Revelation. Last things. The return of Christ. Read with care, and with Christ at the center.

---

## Why a Revelation desk

Few books of the Bible are loved more or misused more than Revelation. We made this desk because too many people meet Revelation through scary headlines, charts, and predictions — and never see what the book is actually about.

What it is actually about is **Jesus Christ**. The first verse says so:

> "This is the Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave him to show to his servants the things which must happen soon..." — Revelation 1:1 (WEB)

Not the revelation of a timeline. The Revelation of Jesus Christ.

---

## How we read Revelation

A few simple commitments shape everything we publish here:

**Christ-centered.** Every vision, image, and chapter is held up to the Lamb who is at the center of the book. If our reading lifts up a politician, a nation, or a fear instead of Christ, we are reading it wrong.

**Scripture-grounded.** Revelation is the last book of a long story. We read it in light of the prophets, the gospels, and the rest of the New Testament. The book does not contradict the rest of the Bible.

**No date-setting.** We will not tell you when Jesus is coming back. He has not told us, and many sincere believers have embarrassed the church and hurt people by claiming to know.

> "But of that day and hour no one knows, not even the angels of heaven, but my Father only." — Matthew 24:36 (WEB)

**No newspaper exegesis.** We will not match every event in the news to a verse in Revelation. We will not say a particular leader is the Antichrist.

**Hope, not fear.** Revelation ends with a wedding, a city, and a tree of life. The last word of the Bible is grace.

---

## What you will find here

- Verse-by-verse teaching through the book.
- Short articles on common questions: the seals, the trumpets, the bowls, the millennium, the New Jerusalem.
- A plain-English explanation of the main views Christians have held — without claiming our view is the only one a believer can hold.
- A "what to do with this" application for each chapter — because Revelation was written to be lived, not just diagrammed.

---

## What we will not do

- We will not predict the date of Christ's return.
- We will not identify the Antichrist by name.
- We will not tell you which side of a political fight is "the beast."
- We will not frighten you into giving us money.

---

## The point

The point of Revelation, and the point of this desk, is this: **Jesus reigns. Jesus is coming. The story ends with God dwelling with His people, every tear wiped away.**

Live ready. Live faithful. Live unafraid.

> "He who testifies these things says, 'Yes, I come quickly.' Amen! Yes, come, Lord Jesus." — Revelation 22:20 (WEB)
