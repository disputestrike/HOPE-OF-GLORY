# Doctrinal Basis

> What Hope of Glory Ministry confesses, and what we leave to the local church.

---

Hope of Glory Ministry is a Christian media and discipleship ministry. We affirm the
historic Christian faith as confessed across centuries, languages, and traditions —
the Apostles' Creed, the Nicene-Constantinopolitan Creed (381), and the Chalcedonian
Definition (451). The fuller doctrinal documents that govern every page on this site
live in our [Statement of Faith](/beliefs) and the [doctrinal constitution](/beliefs).

## What we confess

- **The Bible** as the inspired Word of God — written by men carried along by the Holy
  Spirit (2 Peter 1:21), the sole final authority for faith and practice.
- **One God** eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit (the
  Trinity).
- **Jesus Christ** as fully God and fully man, conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of
  the Virgin Mary, crucified under Pontius Pilate for the sins of the world, buried,
  bodily raised on the third day, ascended, reigning, and returning in glory to judge
  the living and the dead.
- **The Holy Spirit** as Lord and Giver of life, sent from the Father (and the Son),
  who indwells, sanctifies, gifts, and empowers believers.
- **Salvation** by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone — apart from
  works (Ephesians 2:8-9), expressed in repentance (Acts 3:19), evidenced by
  obedience and love.
- **Humanity** made in the image of God (Genesis 1:27), fallen in sin (Romans 3:23),
  redeemable only through Christ.
- **The Church** as the body of Christ, universal across time and place, manifested
  in the local gathering of baptized believers under faithful leadership, practicing
  the ordinances of baptism and the Lord's Supper.
- **The return of Christ**, the bodily resurrection of the dead, the final judgment,
  and the new creation — when the earth will be filled with the knowledge of the glory
  of the Lord (Habakkuk 2:14).

## Where we hold the line

We will not depart from the historic confession of the church on:

- The deity of Christ
- The Trinity
- Salvation by grace through faith in Christ alone
- The authority of Scripture
- The bodily resurrection of Jesus
- The personhood and dignity of every human being

These are non-negotiable.

## Where we welcome charitable disagreement

We do not bind consciences where Scripture has not bound them. On the following
matters, faithful Christians have differed throughout church history. We teach what
Scripture says clearly, name the historic positions charitably, and point believers
to their local church for further conversation:

- Mode and subjects of baptism (immersion, pouring; infants, professing believers)
- Forms of church government (episcopal, presbyterian, congregational)
- Timing of end-time events (pre-, post-, a-millennial; pre-, mid-, post-trib)
- The continuation or cessation of certain spiritual gifts
- Sacramental theology (Lord's Supper — memorial, spiritual presence, real presence)
- Women in ministry roles (complementarian, egalitarian)
- Christian liberty issues (food, drink, days, dress)
- Predestination and free will (Reformed, Wesleyan, Lutheran nuances)
- Age of the earth (young earth, old earth, framework, day-age)

On these matters, we say: *Scripture is clear on X. On Y, faithful Christians have
disagreed. We do not bind your conscience. Talk to your pastor.*

## What this means for AI assistance

The AI tools on this site — including Ask Hope — are bound by this confession. They
will not invent fresh doctrines, contradict the Nicene faith, or take partisan
positions on matters where Scripture is not explicit. When asked about secondary
disputes, they will fairly summarize the major Christian positions and decline to
crown a winner.

When the AI fails — and AI does fail — we tell you. See [Corrections](/corrections).
