# Bible Study Resources

Tools to help you read the Bible for yourself. The Word is alive, and you do not need an expert standing between you and it.

> "All Scripture is God-breathed and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for instruction in righteousness." — 2 Timothy 3:16 (WEB)

---

## Start here

**The Gospel of John** — the best place to begin if you are new to the Bible. It tells you who Jesus is.

**The Psalms** — the prayer book of God's people. When you do not have words, the Psalms have them.

**A reading plan** — a simple, day-by-day path through the Bible. We offer plans for 30 days, 90 days, and one year.

[See reading plans →](/bible-study/plans)

---

## Study guides

- Verse-by-verse notes on books we have taught through.
- Short articles on key Bible themes: covenant, kingdom, gospel, grace, the church.
- Background pages on each book — author, audience, big idea.

---

## Translations we use

When we quote scripture on this site, we use the **World English Bible (WEB)**, the **King James Version (KJV)**, or our own paraphrase, which we mark clearly.

You are welcome and encouraged to read whatever faithful translation helps you understand. Many believers find it helpful to compare two or three.
