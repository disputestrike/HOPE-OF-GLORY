# Crisis Disclaimer

> Read this if you are in danger, or if someone you love is.

---

## The most important sentence on this page

**Hope of Glory Ministry is not emergency care. We cannot replace 911, 988, a hospital,
a crisis counselor, a domestic-violence shelter, or a local emergency responder.**

If you are in immediate danger, please call **[911](tel:911)** — or your country's
equivalent — right now.

If you are having thoughts of suicide or self-harm, please call or text
**[988](tel:988)** (U.S. & Canada Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) right now.

For shelter, food, utility, and community-resource help in the U.S., dial
**[211](tel:211)**.

See our [Crisis resources page](/help/crisis-resources) for international hotlines,
domestic violence, substance use, sexual assault, child protection, and shelter
locators.

## What we are

We are a Christian media and discipleship ministry. We offer:

- Scripture, prayer, and teaching
- Honest answers to honest questions through Ask Hope, our AI chat
- A line you can call for prayer (the Hope Line — when active)
- Pastoral triage that points you to the right next step
- A community of believers around the world

## What we are not

We are not:

- A licensed counselor, psychologist, psychiatrist, doctor, nurse, or therapist
- A pastor, priest, elder, or spiritual director
- A 24/7 crisis response service
- A lawyer or legal advisor
- A shelter, social-service agency, or emergency responder

The AI Scripture-and-prayer companion (Ask Hope) and the AI voice on the Hope Line are
**not human**. They are tools that help us serve more people. They are not equipped
to walk anyone through an active crisis alone.

## When AI shifts into crisis mode

If our systems detect that you are at risk — talking about ending your life, hurting
yourself, being abused, in immediate danger — they will:

1. Stop the normal conversational flow.
2. Tell you, plainly, that you should not be alone with this right now.
3. Surface the right emergency resources for your region.
4. Urge you to contact a trusted person near you.
5. Offer a short prayer.
6. Not continue a long theological discussion while you are in danger.
7. Not promise secrecy.

This is not because we don't care. It is because we care more than we are able to
help with code and text.

## Limits of what we know

- We don't always know where you are physically.
- We don't always know what country you are in.
- We can't always reach you back.
- We don't have your medical history.
- We don't have your family or local support context.
- We can't dispatch help directly to your location.

For anything time-sensitive, the local human responder near you is always the better
first call.

## If you have lost someone to suicide

We are so sorry. The grief is real, and it is yours to carry, but you do not have to
carry it alone. See our pages on [grief](/help/grief), [hopelessness](/help/hopelessness),
and [confession](/help/confession). When you are ready, we are here.

## In short

> Please use the emergency line first. Please reach the trusted person nearby. Please
> call 988 if you are at risk. We are here when you are safe. The Lord is near to the
> brokenhearted — even in the moments when you cannot feel him.
