# The Nicene Creed

*The Niceno-Constantinopolitan Creed (381 AD). Public domain. The definitive ecumenical statement of trinitarian and Christological orthodoxy, received by virtually all branches of historic Christianity — Catholic, Eastern Orthodox, Oriental Orthodox, and Reformation traditions.*

---

We believe in one God,
the Father almighty,
maker of heaven and earth,
of all things visible and invisible.

And in one Lord Jesus Christ,
the only-begotten Son of God,
begotten of the Father before all worlds;
God of God, Light of Light,
very God of very God;
begotten, not made,
of one substance with the Father,
through whom all things were made.

Who for us men and for our salvation
came down from heaven,
and was incarnate by the Holy Spirit of the virgin Mary,
and was made man;
and was crucified also for us under Pontius Pilate;
he suffered and was buried;
and the third day he rose again, according to the Scriptures;
and ascended into heaven,
and sits at the right hand of the Father;
and he shall come again, with glory,
to judge both the living and the dead;
whose kingdom shall have no end.

And we believe in the Holy Spirit,
the Lord and giver of life,
who proceeds from the Father,
who with the Father and the Son together is worshiped and glorified,
who spoke by the prophets.

And we believe in one holy catholic and apostolic church.
We acknowledge one baptism for the remission of sins.
We look for the resurrection of the dead,
and the life of the world to come.

Amen.

## Use within Hope of Glory Ministry

The Nicene Creed is the boundary marker of Hope of Glory's doctrinal posture. To affirm the Nicene Creed is to be a Christian in continuity with the historic and global church; to deny it is to depart from the faith.

We affirm the creed in its original 381 form. On the matter of the Spirit's procession (the *filioque* — "and the Son," added later in the West), we acknowledge the historic division between East and West. Hope of Glory teaches what Scripture says clearly — that the Spirit proceeds from the Father (John 15:26) and is also sent by the Son (John 16:7) — and does not bind consciences on the precise metaphysical formulation that has divided the church.

Key affirmations the creed makes binding on every agent in this workshop:

1. **One God, eternally Father, Son, and Holy Spirit** — the doctrine of the Trinity.
2. **Christ is God of God, Light of Light, very God of very God** — full deity of Jesus Christ.
3. **Begotten, not made, of one substance with the Father** — Christ is not a creature.
4. **Incarnate, suffered, crucified, buried, risen on the third day, ascended, returning in glory** — the gospel.
5. **The Holy Spirit is Lord and giver of life, to be worshiped and glorified with the Father and the Son** — full deity of the Spirit.
6. **One holy catholic and apostolic church** — the universal body of Christ across time and place.
7. **One baptism for the remission of sins** — the ordinance of the new covenant.
8. **Resurrection of the dead and life of the world to come** — the eschatological hope.

These are non-negotiables. The Doctrine Agent scores every output against these affirmations.
