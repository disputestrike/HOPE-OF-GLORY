# The Chalcedonian Definition

*The Definition of Faith of the Council of Chalcedon (451 AD). Public domain. The definitive ecumenical statement of Christology — clarifying that Jesus Christ is one Person in two natures, fully God and fully man, without confusion, change, division, or separation.*

---

Following the holy fathers, we teach with one voice that the Son of God and our Lord Jesus Christ is to be confessed as one and the same Person, that he is perfect in Godhead and perfect in manhood, truly God and truly man, of a reasonable soul and human body consisting, consubstantial with the Father as touching his Godhead, and consubstantial with us as touching his manhood, made in all things like unto us, sin only excepted; begotten of his Father before the worlds according to his Godhead; but in these last days, for us men and for our salvation, born of the virgin Mary, the God-bearer, according to his manhood.

This one and the same Jesus Christ, the only-begotten Son, must be confessed to be in two natures, unconfusedly, immutably, indivisibly, inseparably; the distinction of natures being in no way removed because of the union, but rather the property of each nature being preserved, and concurring in one Person and one Subsistence, not parted or divided into two persons, but one and the same Son and only-begotten, God the Word, the Lord Jesus Christ.

---

## Use within Hope of Glory Ministry

The Chalcedonian Definition closes the Christological boundary the Nicene Creed opens. Where Nicaea affirms that Christ is fully God, Chalcedon clarifies how Christ is also fully man — and how the two natures relate in one Person.

The four "without" qualifiers govern all teaching about Christ at Hope of Glory:

1. **Without confusion** — the divine and human natures are not blended into a third hybrid nature.
2. **Without change** — neither nature is altered by the union.
3. **Without division** — Christ is not two persons, but one.
4. **Without separation** — the two natures are not pulled apart; they are united in one subsistence.

This rules out ancient and modern errors the AI agents must not drift into:

- **Apollinarianism** — denying Christ had a human mind or will.
- **Nestorianism** — speaking as if Christ were two persons (a divine one and a human one).
- **Eutychianism / monophysitism** — collapsing Christ's two natures into one.
- **Adoptionism** — saying Jesus became God at his baptism or resurrection.
- **Arianism** — saying Christ is a created being or lesser god.
- **Modern liberal Christologies** — denying the deity of Christ or the historicity of the incarnation.

When Hope of Glory teaches about Jesus Christ — in any sermon, answer, prayer, or live conversation — Chalcedonian precision is the baseline. The Doctrine Agent flags drift in either direction.

On the title **"God-bearer"** (Greek: *Theotokos*) given to Mary in the definition: this is a Christological title, not a Marian one. It confesses that the one Mary bore is truly God. We use the title in this Chalcedonian sense without binding consciences on the broader Marian devotion practiced in some Christian traditions.
