# The Apostles' Creed

*A summary of the apostolic faith. Public domain. Used across Western Christianity since the 4th century in its earliest forms, with the received text dating to the 8th century.*

---

I believe in God, the Father almighty,
creator of heaven and earth.

I believe in Jesus Christ, his only Son, our Lord,
who was conceived by the Holy Spirit
and born of the virgin Mary.
He suffered under Pontius Pilate,
was crucified, died, and was buried;
he descended to the dead.
On the third day he rose again;
he ascended into heaven,
he is seated at the right hand of the Father,
and he will come to judge the living and the dead.

I believe in the Holy Spirit,
the holy catholic Church,
the communion of saints,
the forgiveness of sins,
the resurrection of the body,
and the life everlasting.

Amen.

---

## Use within Hope of Glory Ministry

The Apostles' Creed is one of three ecumenical creeds the ministry receives as faithful summaries of biblical doctrine. It is not Scripture, but it is a true summary of what Scripture teaches concerning the triune God, the person and work of Christ, and the hope of resurrection life.

When we say "holy catholic Church," we use the word *catholic* in its original sense — meaning *universal*, the whole body of Christ across history, language, and place — not the Roman Catholic Church specifically.

When we say "he descended to the dead," we receive this in the historic sense: that Christ truly died, was truly buried, and entered fully into the realm of the dead before being raised on the third day. We do not bind consciences on speculative views beyond this.

The creed is summarized in three articles, corresponding to the three persons of the Trinity:
- Father — Creator
- Son — Redeemer (incarnate, crucified, risen, ascended, returning)
- Holy Spirit — Sanctifier (and the church he gathers, the forgiveness he applies, the resurrection he secures)

This structure governs how Hope of Glory teaches the gospel: from the Father, through the Son, in the Spirit.
